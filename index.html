Introduction:

Cyber Security and young people. (SFW)

Your routine

Cyber Security is important for everyone, however teaching such an in-depth and ever changing subject to children and young adults can be challenging, the aim of this website is to supplement this process by giving up-to-date information on a wide variety of categories relating to Cyber Security and the prevention of security breaches and malicious attacks.
These demographics are amongst the heaviest users of the internet but also generally have a lower level of understanding of security risks and common malicious practices, this means that younger people on the internet are massively at risk, and it is important to educate them on how to avoid and prevent instances of security breaches.


Treat your online classes and work as you would do in person

One commonly found way to succeed at online study is to treat the time as you would do in person. This means treating your active study time as if you were in a real class, no phones whilst teaching is in prgress, don't chat with other occupants and anything thats would be considered taboo in an in-person teaching setting. Turning off non work mobile devices and muting social media is also important to maximise attenstion and minimise distractions.
Although the idea of studying from home is enticing when you think about attending classes from your bed or playing computer games whilst a lesson is in progress, it is enormously counter productive to your studies and its a all too common mistake made by new students.


Stay Disciplined

Another aspect of studying from home is self discipline, meaning you should treat a course as you would if you were attending it in-person. You should alighn your study schedule to that of your course so you are always available for live lectures, rather than their recorded counterparts allowing you to ask questions in person should you have them.
You should also find it useful to have segregated work and rest spaces, ideally these being entirely different rooms and not just a desk in a bedroom or living room. This will eliminate the feeling of always being stuck in your workspace and is important for maintaining optimal attention.
You will also need to set time aside to complete any out of class work you are set and undertake any revision for exams, ideally you will have all of this incorporated intor your daily or weekly routine from an early stage as starting on the work early eliminates the last minute rush before hand-in students often find themselves in.


Look after yourself

Aside from full-time studying, it is also important to have time off and make plans to go out and relax for periods of time so as to not become overwhelmed with studies.
Taking lunch breaks is also vital, especially on days where you have a full timetable, keeping your energy and in turn attention levels high are necessary to successfully undertake ontine training.
If you would like some further tips, 20 Tips for working from home has plenty more ideas for you to maximise your effectiveness when learning from home.
